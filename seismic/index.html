In Niagara Falls, Ontario, comprehensive seismic site investigation is the critical first step for any project on the complex glacial stratigraphy of the Niagara Peninsula. Our services address the specific seismic hazards outlined in the Ontario Building Code (OBC 2012, Division B, Part 4), which directly references the National Building Code of Canada (NBC 2020). We characterize the overburden soils—predominantly glacial till, glaciolacustrine clays from the former Lake Iroquois plain, and fluvial sands—to determine Site Class for seismic design. A precise geotechnical investigation is essential to quantify the potential for ground motion amplification in these soft soil deposits overlying the competent Queenston Shale and Lockport Dolostone bedrock, directly impacting the seismic force-resisting system of your structure.

Our field methodology adheres strictly to Canadian Standards Association (CSA) and ASTM International standards to generate defensible, code-compliant data. We deploy cone penetration testing (CPT) to provide a continuous, high-resolution profile of soil behaviour type, which is crucial for liquefaction assessment in saturated sandy layers near the Niagara River. This is complemented by In-Situ, including seismic shear wave velocity (Vs) measurements via downhole or crosshole methods, a direct requirement for accurate Site Class determination per NBC Table 4.1.8.4.A. For Improvement and fill control, we perform nuclear densometer and sand cone density testing to verify compaction meets project specifications for mitigating seismic settlement.

Our laboratory testing program is calibrated to the unique seismic demands of projects across the Niagara region, from the tourist district near the Falls to industrial and renewable energy installations. To model the cyclic behaviour of fine-grained soils, we conduct precise Atterberg limits testing to establish the plasticity index, a key parameter in liquefaction susceptibility screening of silts and clays. A full grain size analysis, combining mechanical sieving and hydrometer sedimentation, is performed to generate the complete particle-size distribution curve, which is directly correlated with soil liquefaction potential and dynamic properties. These results, integrated with our advanced laboratory testing for shear strength, provide the essential parameters for designing robust deep and shallow foundations that can withstand seismic loading.

Our process delivers a clear, actionable pathway from site characterization to design. We begin with a desktop review of surficial geology and seismic hazard maps, execute a targeted field and laboratory testing program, and synthesize the findings into a factual and interpretive report. The final deliverable provides explicit seismic design parameters, including the Site Class, spectral acceleration values, liquefaction potential index, and recommendations for Improvement or foundation type. For engineers and developers in Niagara Falls, this integrated service transforms complex seismic code requirements into a practical, risk-managed foundation solution, ensuring structural resilience and regulatory approval.
